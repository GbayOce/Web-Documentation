Background

The Australian Koala Foundation is a non-profit, non-government organisation that deals with the conservation and management of wild koala habitat all over Australia. Their role is to provide an outlet for the general public to help koalas in their own way by giving donations, adopting a koala of their own and through fundraising. However this role can be achieved better with a better designed website and coherent theme.

The Need

The Australian Koala Foundation has failed to create a website that; details their actions as an organisation; educate young Australinas on the impact of catastrophic events and its impact on the koalas; entice users into supporting the koala population. There is just a lot of additional pieces of information necessary to create a stronger impression on the user to urge and entice them into donating to the cause.

In light on recent events, the Australian bushfires, CNN published an article" The koala population faces an immediate threat of extinction after the Australia bushfires, new report finds" detailing that a minimum of 5000 koalas are estimated to have died during the catastrophic events. With quotes like:

"Koalas are particularly vulnerable to bushfires as they are slow moving and live in eucalyptus trees that burn quickly and intensely,"

With 12 million acres of land in NSWs burned and 45 million acres in all of Australia, introducing a very relevant and fear mongering statistic, this isn't stated anyway within their current website. The website however does highlight the importance of the Koala Protection Act to prevent Australian governments from destroying koala habitats any further.

"The Koala Protection Act will also insist that the Koala should be treated with immense respect, and that every single tree on the Australian landscape needs to be protected; not only for the Koalas, but the millions of other species living there, including humans."

However this does not garner the sympathy and sentiment of users and therefore requires some design changes. To understand what makes a good charity organisation, we must first find out what type of people donate to charities and their reason for doing so.

User Profiles

The layout and design of the Australian Koala Foundation's homepage should be targeted towards those who are likely to either donate or spark inspiration from young Australians to save the koalas. This can be done through 2 ways, education and entertainment. By making the website interesting, entertaining and ludic, the users are more likely to donate to the cause.

According to a study done by Nivea Coelho Degasperi, What motivates money donation? A study on external motivators he has discovered the most important variables that prompts users to donate are:

  • trust in the organisation
  • credibility of the organisation
  • trust in the organisation leadership
  • catastrophe
  • consciousness of need
  • image of the organisation

Based on this criteria, the Australian Koala Foundation is doing a huge injustice from themselves not capitalising on the catastrophe that was the Australian bushfires while also degrading the other aspects in their charity such as credibility and trust.

Ideation

The website landing page should immediately garner attention through playful, ludic design while contrasted with the grim current events to invoke feelings of sympathy, sentiment and pity for the koalas. It should also aim to make learning about koalas interesting and fun, adding small animations and a better colour scheme would drastically aid the website to be better.

Mood Boards

As the goal of the Australian Koala Foundation's website is to garner sympathy and sentiment while also enticing students and young australians into educating themselves There needs to be a balance of cuteness and shock marketing. Contrasting the two, the web design needs to find a balance between them, to not provoke or stun the user instead to inspire and educate.
